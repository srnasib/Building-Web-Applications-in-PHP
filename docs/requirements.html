Basic Prototype

The Clever Swabian

14 May 2019

Welcome to the blog of team The Clever Swabian! We are a team of four students who are excited to show you the progress of our project. We are currently building our first Android application and have finished a basic prototype. On this page, we are going to inform you about the steps we have taken to this point.

Requirement analysis

At the beginning of the course project, we as a group already discussed about a possible realisation of the basic requirements that were initially given. In order to get a feeling of what features a money control app shall encompass, we also took feedback from different types of people who didn’t attend the ISEE lecture but might be interested in such an app. This included other students as well as regular job holders and their typical ways of expensing money. During the following weekly meetings with our customer Dr. Schulze, we checked if our understanding of the requirements was correct and which features were explicitly desired by the customer for which release. When we had translated an abstract requirement into a certain way of implementing it or when we imagined features that would fit well into the context, we asked the customer in the next meeting about those ideas. As we also showed central documentation pieces that were finished in the past week to him, we could always assure that we don’t develop our app into a direction that would be non-conform from the customer’s perspective.

In our weekly scrum meetings right after the exchange with Dr. Schulze on Thursdays, we discussed what tasks would have to be derived from the given input. In the first phase of the project, a documentation was set up step by step from which a proper app could be built on. When we realised that our understanding of the project has changed, we backtracked the respective documents and brought them up to date.

The first document that resulted from this process was the list of requirements. Therein, we sorted the information by two criteria: the importance and the context within the app. For the first criterion, we sorted our requirements by either “essential”, “required” and “desired”. Implementation

After we finished the documentation and had already played around with “Hello world” like apps in Android Studio, we were ready to get our hands dirty on implementing the essential features of our app. We tackled this process in a dynamic fashion. Instead of discussing requirements anymore, we now slice the planned features into feasible portions and assign the implementation work of those to the team members during our weekly meetings. The assigned tasks are put to protocol and uploaded to our G-Drive. When a task has been finished – especially when other tasks are dependent of it – or when team member has met a problem, we immediately discuss about it on Messenger. In the following team meetings, we analyse whether our weekly planning has been successful and if not, for what reasons and how the process could be improved.

Finally, this initial implementation led to a working first prototype. When the app is opened for the first time, an “empty” home screen will welcome the user. But by adding entries, it will display soon the current balance and recent entries:

An entry can be added by accessing the corresponding tab and specifying the desired information including the type, the amount, a title, the date, the category, the payment method and a longer comment if needed.

As the home screen only shows the last three entries, all entries can be accessed in the scrollable list overview tab:

Entries are saved to a hidden file and thus are loaded properly again when the app gets restarted. As it took is quite some time to figure out how to code XML files for the layout and get to know with basic Android coding, we didn’t finish implementing the planned recurring entry feature and the currency selection. But as we now have a much improved knowledge about app development, we are confident to implement these features very soon. The APK file to install the app can be downloaded here.
