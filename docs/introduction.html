Our app

The app "The clever Swabian" will allow users to keep track of their daily financial transactions. Thus, it helps to keep a positive balance and identify unnecessary spending while not requiring big documentation efforts.

You may ask yourself, what our name stands for. A common stereotype among Germans is that the people from the southwestern German region Swabia are rather frugal. This means that they prefer saving money instead of spending it wherever it is possible. If you are a clever steoretypical Swabian, you might not want to abolish this trait of yours but rather bring it to the digital age for which we are delivering the appropriate tools.

Motivation behind our App

It is often necessary for us to keep our budget in check in every month. As humans no one is above temptations so often it is difficult to track down on which purpose we have spent after the month is over. So this is an app which will help us to organize our spending and savings. In this way it will keep us focused on our monthly money goals. Being student means we always have limited financial resources so we wanted to built an app which will be helpful in our real life and make an impact in our practical life, that's another we have chosen this app as our project.

Communication within the team

Weekly meetings will be held during the designated periods. Furthermore, if problems arise and need to be discussed face-to-face, we will agree on a flexible meeting time and do our best to solve them! For all kinds of organisational purposes, we have established a group chat. In this way, every participant is always up-to-date about the state of the project. Also, everyone can express their ideas on improvements and changes. We intend to use Messenger to exchange ideas, G-Drive to share files and TeamViewer to control remote machines and make the best use of technological tools available.

Responsibilities

In the upcoming first sprint of our project, tasks like the exact analysis of the app's user requirements, creating a first mock-up of the logical design and eventually coding a working first prototype have to be focused on. As all of us intend to learn as much software engineering related content as possible, each team member will get his hands dirty on most tasks over the project's timespan. However, in order to work as efficiently as possible in the first weeks, we have assigned primary responsibilities for the first sprint (which doesn't mean that the others won't contribute to these fields). We agreed on these responsibilities:

  • Muhib: Requirement analysis and logic system design
  • Shaiful: Basis UI design and layout
  • Shohanoor: Initial implementation
  • Philipp: Quality assurance of the mentioned fields

Reacting to changing circumstances

Scrum is an agile process that allows us to focus on delivering the highest business value in the shortest time. As described, by keeping up a vital communication, we will be able to react to constant changes in the best way possible – especially in the agile way of Scrum.

With the help of the online tool GitHub we will be able to track the project and every team member will be updated by having access to the most recent files.

Conclusion

We are confident that the foundation for a successful project has been laid out and we are ready to start building our app. For the next step, we are eager to meet with our customer (Dr. Sandro Schulze) to discuss the detailed requirements.
